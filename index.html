Why?

Halo Pro is a niche application that focuses on halation precision and creative expression.

Other applications like Dehancer and Filmic do a lot of steps to emulate film, but Halo Pro specializes on the hardest part of emulating the film look — the halation effect. The soft glowy edges adding range and a magic feel to the image.

Furthermore, other software is only 8-bit precision which works great for film but for photo it's simply not matching the quality photographers expect. With 32-bit float, the gradients and blurred colors get integrated into the image rather than slapped on. 32-bit also allows you to add halation at any time in your workflow — you're not limited to adding it as the last step before exporting.

The project was started by solo developer and professional photographer Alric Ljunghager in 2024, entered beta in February 2026, and is explicitly built for M-series macOS computers. Expected to remain in beta until 2027, with Windows/CUDA support planned.

Core Features

Halation Engine

Multi-pass processing with variable spread and color channel control. Independent adjustments for highlight threshold, diffusion radius, and chromatic response.

Mist & Diffusion

Optical diffusion simulation including bloom characteristics, contrast reduction mapping, and film-accurate softening curves.

Film Response

Complete signal chain with highlight rolloff, toe compression, and grain modeling. LUT integration for established workflows.

Precision Masking

Non-destructive luminosity and color range selection with feathering controls. Isolate effects to specific tonal ranges.
